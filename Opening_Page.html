Interview with

Alice Bowsher

When I, Nicholas Mendoza, browse my email at midweek, I usually get a weekly email from a newsletter called Midweek Mentor provided by It's Nice That. Every midweek they release a short interview with an indie designer, talking about design tips, favorite designers to follow on Instagram, and what they wanted to become as a six-year-old before being part of the creative industry. The point of the midweek newsletter was to inspire designers with advice and inspiration.

On this website, I recreate the transcripted interview of Alice Bowsher, an illustrator I discovered in one of the emails on March 17, 2021. Her illustration style is playful and childish—Bowsher is an author of some children's books. I also find it charming how she uses black ink to create texture in her illustrations—it makes me reminisce about the illustrated children's books I read before I slept.

The illustrations in picture books are the first paintings most children see, and because of that, they are incredibly important. What we see and share at the age stays with us for life.

— Anthony Browne

Thank you for reading, whoever you may be! Hope you are inspired just as I am!
